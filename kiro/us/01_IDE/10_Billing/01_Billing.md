# Billing for Individuals

> **Source:** [kiro.dev/docs/billing/](https://kiro.dev/docs/billing/)

---

Kiro uses a credit-based billing system with multiple tiers designed for individual developers. All plans include access to the core IDE features; tiers differ by credit allocation and model availability.

---

## Service Tiers

| Tier | Monthly Credits | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | Limited | Evaluation and light use |
| **Pro** | ~1,000 credits | Active individual developers |
| **Pro+** | Higher limit | Power users with heavy usage |
| **Power** | Highest limit | Professional / high-volume users |

> Refer to [kiro.dev/pricing](https://kiro.dev/pricing) for exact credit limits and current pricing.

---

## Billing Practices

### Upgrading from Free → Paid (first time)

If you are on the **Kiro Free tier**, have never upgraded before, and upgrade to Pro, Pro+, or Power mid-month:
- You are charged a **prorated fee** for the remainder of the month
- You immediately get access to your new plan's **full credit limits**

If you upgrade again in the same month (e.g., Free → Pro on April 15th, then Pro → Pro+ on April 20th):
- Fees are **backdated proportionally** as if you had chosen the higher tier from day one
- After that, you'll be charged the full amount going forward

### Upgrading Between Paid Tiers

If you are already on a paid tier and upgrade to a higher paid tier mid-month:
- You are charged a **retroactive fee for the entire month** based on the price difference
- Kiro recalculates the entire month's usage under the new tier's credits

> **Example:** You used 1,010 credits (10 over the Pro cap) and then upgraded to Pro+. The overage charge is eliminated because your usage falls under the Pro+ limit.

> **Note:** If you're on a paid tier, switch to Free, and then switch back to a paid tier, the payment behavior is the same as upgrading from Free → Paid.

### Downgrading

- Downgrades **take effect in the following month** (upgrades are effective immediately)
- You are charged the **full fee for the current month** even if you downgrade mid-month

> **Example:** If you downgrade from Pro+ to Pro on June 2nd, you are charged the full Pro+ subscription fee for June. From July onward, you are charged the Pro fee.

### Overages

- If you exceed your monthly credit limit, you are charged the total overage fees **at the end of the billing cycle**

### Credit Renewal

- For **all tiers**, your usage cap renews at the beginning of the **next billing cycle**
